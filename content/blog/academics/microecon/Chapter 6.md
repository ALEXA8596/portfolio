# Supply, Demand, and Government Policies

### 6-1. The Surprising Effects of Price Controls
- Price ceilings: regulations put in place that prevents a good's price from going above a certain level
- Price floor: regulations put in place that prevents a good's price from going below a certain level.
- Not binding: when a price ceiling is placed above the equilibrium price, or when a price floor is placed below the equilibrium price.
- Binding constraint: when a price ceiling or floor affects the market price.
- Price ceilings may cause shortages
	- Waitlists
	- Rationing
- "When the government imposes a binding price ceiling on a competitive market, a shortage arises, and sellers must ration scarce goods among potential buyers"

### 6-1b. How Price Floors Affect Market Outcomes
- A binding price floor causes a surplus.

### 6-1c. Evaluating Price Controls
- Markets can generally function without intervention thanks to the invisible hand
- However, governments can step in to promote equality.
- However, those efforts can fail, such as rent control.
	- Instead, countries can subsidize or pay for the rent themselves

### 6-2. The Surprising Study of Tax Incidence
- Tax Incidence
	- Who should pay the tax? The seller or the buyer?

### 6-2a. How Taxes on Sellers Affect Market Outcomes
- Taxes on Sellers shifts Supply to the left.
- Sellers make less and buyers pay more.

### 6-2b. How Taxes on Buyers Affect Market Outcomes
- Taxes on Buyers shifts Demand to the left.
- The same situation occurs here too, and sellers make less and buyers pay more.

### 6-2c. Elasticity and Tax Incidence
- When the supply is more elastic, the tax burden is placed on the buyers
- When the demand is more elastic, the tax burden is placed on the sellers.
- "A tax burden falls more heavily on the side of the market that is less elastic."
