# Thinking Like an Economist
### 2-1
- Economics is a science, and Economists are scientists.
	- They analyze data, devise theories, follow the scientific method.
- Scientific Method
	- Economists make observations and then make theories
	- However, Economists can't really do experiments
		- Instead, they pay close attention to certain events, such as oil crises.
- Economists must also make assumptions
	- Just like physicists assume gravity = 10m/s^2 or wind resistance is negligible
	- We might assume that prices don't change in the short run, but are completely flexible in the long run
- Economists use models such as charts, diagrams, and equations to make predictions.
	- These models are built with assumptions
- [[The Circular Flow Diagram]]
	- The diagram describes buying, selling, working, manufacturing, hiring, etc.
	- There are 2 decision makers: firms and households
	- There are 2 markets
		- markets for goods and services
			- Where firms sell and households buy
		- markets for factors of production
			- Where firms hire and households work

![[Pasted image 20260714201531.png]]

- The Production Possibilities Frontier
	- A graph that shows the possible production capabilities of an economy for two specific goods / services.
	- Displays opportunity costs
	- An economy is efficient if it is on the curve
	- An economy is inefficient if it is below the curve

![[Pasted image 20260714203039.png]]

- Microeconomics is the study of how households and firms make decisions and how those decisions impact specific markets
- Macroeconomics is the study of the overall economy

### 2-2
- Economists are also policy advisors
- When asked, they must provide insights as to how to improve the economy.

- Positive v Normative
	- Positive statements are based on how the world is
	- Normative statements are based on how the world should be

- Economics is not always straight forward
	- Economic advisors must always make tradeoffs
		- i.e. equality over efficiency
- Economists are all over the government
	- The white house is advised by the Council of Economic Advisers
	- The Office of Management and Budget help formulate spending plans and regulatory policies
	- The Department of Treasury design tax policies
	- The Department of Labor analyzes worker data
	- The Department of Justice enforce antitrust laws
	- The Congressional Budget Office sets the budget with the help of economists
	- The Federal Reserve sets the nation's monetary policy

- Leaders don't always follow what the economists say.
	- They must also weigh the public's opinions, the effects, the media's opinion, etc.
	- Congress must approve, which means that politics must be considered.

- Economists disagree with each other; There is no one correct objective truth.
	- They disagree on which theories apply and which values to prioritize
	- They disagree on the parameters that measure the economy
	- For example: whether to income tax or sales tax.
	- They have different values
		- i.e. tax brackets vs universal percentage (equality vs equity vs justice)

- Economists say that rent control is bad, yet politicians impose rent ceilings.
- Economists say that tariffs are bad, yet politicians regularly impose tariffs.
