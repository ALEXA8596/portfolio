# The Market Forces of Supply and Demand

### 4-1a What Is a Market?
- Market: A group of buyers and sellers of a  good and service
	- Demand is determined by the buyers
	- Supply is determined by the sellers
	- Markets might be organized like a live auction, or it might be decentralized like independent stores in a town.

### 4-1b What Is Competition?
- Competitive Market: A market where there are many buyers and sellers, and the actions of one has little effect on the market price.
- Perfectly Competitive Market: A market in which all the goods are exactly the same and the number of buyers and sellers is so large that one has no influence over the market price.
	- Buyers and sellers are price takers in perfectly competitive markets since they must accept the market price.
- Monopolies are the opposite of perflectly competitive markets in that they can set whatever price they wish

### 4-2 Demand
### 4-2a The Demand Curve: The Relationship between Price and Quantity Demanded
- Quantity Demanded: The amount of a good that the buyers are willing and able to purchase
- The Law of Demand: When the price of a good rises, the quantity demanded falls. When the price of a good falls, the quantity demanded rises.
- Demand Schedule: A table that shows the relationship of a price of a good and the quantity demanded.
- Demand Curve: The Line relating price and quantity demanded
	- Slopes downward
### 4-2b Market Demand versus Individual Demand
- Market Demand: The sum of the demand in a market
- Sum the demand horizontally
	- i.e. add up the demand quantities of each individual demand at each price

### 4-2c Shifts in the Demand Curve
- A shift to the right is an increase (positive) in demand
- A shift to the left is a decrease (negative) in demand

- Determinants of Demand
	- Income
		- When income decreases, demand for normal goods decrease.
		- If demand for a good increases when income decreases, that good is an inferior good.
			- i.e. bus rides over ubers and cars
	- Related Goods
		- Substitutes
			- When substitutes decrease in price, demand for the good decreases.
			- When substitutes increase in price, demand for the good increases.
		- Complements
			- When complements increase in price, demand for the good decreases
			- When complements decrease in price, demand for the good decreases
	- Tastes
		- Whether individuals prefer / like a certain good
	- Expectations
		- Price expectations / Income expectations
	- Number of Buyers
		- If buyers increase, demand increases

### 4-3 Supply
- Quantity Supplied: The amount of a good or service that the sellers are willing and able to sell
- Law of Supply: When a price of a good rises, the quantity supplied also rises. When price falls, the quantity supplied falls.
- Demand Schedule: A table that shows the relationship of a price of a good and the quantity supplied.
- Demand Curve: The line relating price and quantity supplied
	- Slopes upward

### 4-3b Market Supply versus Individual Supply
- Market Supply: The sum of all individual supplies
	- Obtain the market supply by summing the quantity that all suppliers are willing supply at a given price

### 4-3c Shifts in the Supply Curve
- A shift to the right is an increase (positive) in supply
- A shift to the left is a decrease (negative) in supply

- Determinants
	- Input Prices
		- If the inputs needed to make a good (labor, materials) increase, the supply decreases
	- Technology
		- If technology improves and increases production, supply increases.
	- Expectations:
		- If suppliers expect prices to increase, they might store goods to sell later
	- Number of Sellers
		- More sellers lead to more supply.


### 4-4 Supply and Demand Together

- Equilibrium: where the Supply Curve and the Demand Curve intersect.
	- This point has an equilibrium price (market-clearing price) and an equilibrium quantity.
	- This is the price at which the quantity that the buyers are willing to purchase match the quantity that the sellers are willing to sell.

- Surplus: when the market price is above equilibrium (people don't purchase, leading to a surplus)
	- Graphically, when there is a upside down triangle
- Shortage: when the market price is below equilibrium (people purchase too much)
	- Graphically, when there is a triangle

- The Law of Supply and Demand: The price of any good adjusts to bring the quantity supplied and the quantity demanded into balance.
