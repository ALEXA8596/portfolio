# Elasticity

### 5-1 The Elasticity of Demand
- Price Elasticity of Demand: How much the quantity demanded responds to a price change.
	- A good's demand is elastic if it changes substantially
	- A good's demand is inelastic if it changes minimally

- Determinants of Elasticity
	- Goods with close substitutes have more elastic demand since buyers can just go to another substitute.
	- Necessities have inelastic demand
	- Luxuries have elastic demand
	- A narrow or broad market has elastic and inelastic demand
		- i.e. food is broad and is inelastic, ice cream is narrow and is elastic
	- Time Horizon
		- Demand becomes more elastic as time goes on
			- i.e. Gas. If gas prices increase, people will just go to EVs

### 5-1b The Price Elasticity of Demand, with Numbers
- Price elasticity of demand is equal to the percentage change in quantity demand divided by the percentage change in price.
	- Signs
		- Since quantity demanded moves in the opposite direction as its price, price elasticity is negative. However, absolute value is sometimes used instead.

If the quantity moves proportionately more than the price, the elasticity is greater than one, and the demand is said to be elastic.

If the quantity moves proportionately less than the price, the elasticity is less than one, and the demand is said to be inelastic.

If the percentage change in quantity equals the percentage change in price, the elasticity is exactly 1 and the demand has unit elasticity.

Perfect Inelastic Demand is represented by a straight vertical line. (elasticity equals 0)

Perfectly Elastic Demand is represented by a straight horizontal line. (elasticity equals infinity)

A curve between two points is inelastic if the vertical change is greater than the horizontal change.

### 5-1e Total Revenue and the Price Elasticity of Demand
- The total revenue is the price times the quantity
- When price elasticity is greater than 1 (elastic), as price increases, revenue decreases, and as price decreases, revenue increases.
- When price elasticity is less than 1 (inelastic), as price increases, revenue increases, and as price decreases, revenue decreases.
- If elasticity = 1, then the revenue stays the same.

### 5-1f Elasticity and Total Revenue along a Linear Demand Curve

At points with a low price and high quantity, a linear demand curve is inelastic. At points with a high price and low quantity, a linear demand curve is elastic.

The price elasticity of demand need not be the same at all points on a demand curve. A demand curve with constant elasticity is possible, but it is a special case. A linear demand curve never has a constant elasticity.

### 5-1g Other Demand Elasticities
- Income Elasticity of Demand: A measure of how the quantity demanded changes as income changes.
- Income elasticity of demand is equal to the percentage change in quantity demand divided by the percentage change in income.

- Normal goods have positive income elasticities, since higher income leads to increasing purchases.
	- Necessities has smaller income elasticities, since costs mostly stay the same as income increases. (**Engel’s Law**)
	- Luxuries have large income elasticities since as income declines, purchases also decline.
- Inferior goods have negative income elasticities, since quantity demanded and income are opposite directions.

-  cross-price elasticity of demand: A measure of how the change in quantity demanded for one good responds to the change in the price of another good
	- Is positive if substitutes
	- Is negative if complements

### 5-2 The Elasticity of Supply
- Price elasticity of supply: A measure of how much the quantity supplied responds to changes in the price.
	- Elastic if the quantity supplied responds significantly to price changes
	- Inelastic if the quantity supplied responds minimally to price changes
	- Supply is more elastic in the long run than in the short run

### 5-2b The Price Elasticity of Supply, with Numbers
- Price elasticity of supply is equal to the percentage change in quantity supply divided by the percentage change in price.

### 5-2c The Variety of Supply Curves
- Perfectly Inelastic Supply is when the supply curve is vertical (e = 0).
- Perfectly Elastic Supply is when the supply curve is horizontal (e = infinity).
