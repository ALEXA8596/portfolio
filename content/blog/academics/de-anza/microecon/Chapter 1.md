# The Ten Principles of Economics
### 1-1a. Principle 1: People Face Trade-Offs
- People must decide how to allocate time and money
- [[Efficiency]]: when a society is gaining the most benefits for its scarce resources
- [[Equality]]: when a society's benefits are distributed equally among society's members
### 1-1b. Principle 2: The Cost of Something Is What You Give Up to Get It
- [[Opportunity Cost]]: What is given up to do something
### 1-1c. Principle 3: Rational People Think at the Margin
- Rational people try to do the best with their resources (i.e. maximize profits)
	- They compare marginal benefits and marginal costs

### 1-1d. Principle 4: People Respond to Incentives
- An incentive induces a person to act (punishment or reward)
	- i.e. higher prices dissuade customers
- Policymakers may consider subsidies and taxes

### 1-2a. Principle 5: Trade Can Make Everyone Better Off
- Trade benefits all parties
	- Countries can specialize at making the goods and services they are best at.

### 1-2b. Principle 6: Markets Are Usually a Good Way to Organize Economic Activity
- In Communism, government officials became the central planners in the economy who decide how the country's resources should be allocated.
- In Market Economies, the central planners are replaced by the firms and households who act in their own self interest.
	- Adam Smith: "Firms and households in competitive markets act as if they are guided by an “invisible hand” that leads them to desirable outcomes."
	- Usually, the invisible hand is price.
	- Taxes distort prices, and thus negatively impact the market economy

### 1-2c. Principle 7: Governments Can Sometimes Improve Market Outcomes
- Governments are able to positively impact the economy.
- Governments must enforce property rights
- Governments can intervene to promote one of two ideas: equality or efficiency.
- Issues around efficiency
	- Market Failure: when a market is unable to attain efficient allocation of materials on its own
		- One cause is Externality: When one's actions has an effect on bystanders
			- i.e. Pollution
		- Another cause is market power: the ability of a single person or firm to influence market prices
			- i.e. Monopolies
- Issues around Equality
	- The invisible hand leaves financial disparities
	- The government may address equality through income tax and the welfare system.
	- Has flaws, such as corruption and incompetent leaders.
### 1-3a. Principle 8: A Country’s Standard of Living Depends on Its Ability to Produce Goods and Services
- Different countries have different median income levels. This reflects the quality of life of each nation.
- Differences in living standards across countries is based on their different productivity levels.
	- Productivity: The amount of goods and services (g/s) produced by each unit of labor input.

### 1-3b. Principle 9: Prices Rise When the Government Prints Too Much Money
- Inflation: an increase in the overall level of prices in a given economy
- The cause is almost always too much printing or too much spending.

### 1-3c. Principle 10: Society Faces a Short-Run Trade-Off between Inflation and Unemployment
- In the long run, an increase in the quantity of money increases prices
- In the short run, however, increasing money in the economy stimulates it, increasing demand for g/s.
	- This encourages firms to hire more employees and produce more goods.
- Thus: a short-run trade-off between inflation and unemployment.
- This is the Phillips Curve
